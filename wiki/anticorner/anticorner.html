Anti-corner

Integer Parition Combinatorial Game

Anticorner is played on a Young diagram. An "anti-corner" is a cell where the shape dips inward — it has no cell to its right and no cell below it (the topmost-right cell and the bottommost-left cell of the whole shape always count as anti-corners too). On your turn, pick an anti-corner and remove it together with every cell to its right in its row and every cell below it in its column. This is an impartial game — the player who makes the last move wins.
